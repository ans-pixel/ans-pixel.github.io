The mammalian brain has three fundamental parts: the cerebrum, the cerebellum, and the brain stem (consisting of the pons and the medulla). These three regions are functionally distinct, with the cerebrum being responsible for higher cognitive function and is also the seat for sensory process ing. The cerebellum is the brain structure associated with coordinating motor functions and mainta ining balance. The brainstem region hosts the medulla which is responsible for performing numerous involuntary functions such as respiration.

The cerebrum is the largest aspect of the brain and contains numerous structures involved in variou s functions, ranging from somatosensory input processing to motor planning and output processing. T he outer layer of the cerebrum is the cerebral cortex, which itself consists of two parts: the neoc ortex and the allocortex. The neocortex was found to be organised in a laminar fashion, with its ne urons organised into 6 layers.
